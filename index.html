Bio
I am a current PhD student at MIT advised by Pulin Li, a member of the Whitehead Institute. My research focuses on how we represent biological perturbations problems meaningfully in AI/ML approaches and create tools and approaches that illuminate aspects of biology that were not previously possible. Much of my graduate work has focused on how we predictively model stem cell engineering and identify which combinations of perturbations drive cells into desired states. I am broadly interested in expanding and using the uncertainty quantification tool box to better understand the properties of ML models in biology. I have enjoyed working with many collaborators throughout my PhD, most notably at Kira Learning, GC Therapeutics, and Microsoft Research New England.
I am fortunate to be supported by an MIT Presidential Fellowship, an NSF Graduate Research Fellowship, a Bryll Graduate Fellowship and a Whitehead Institute Innovation Award.
Before starting my PhD, I was a research associate at Twist Bioscience and received my BS from Caltech.
